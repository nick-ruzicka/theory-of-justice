## 2. KEY POINTS OF DISAGREEMENT

### The Fundamental Fault Line: **Epsilon's Categorical Rejection**

**Epsilon's position** (isolated, unwavering): Zero mandatory baseline without explicit individual consent. Views any compulsory redistribution as morally equivalent to theft, regardless of circumstances behind the veil.

**Core impasse**: Epsilon treats libertarian moral convictions as a "position" equivalent to disability, poverty, or geographic bad luck—asking "why does the disabled person's claim trump the moral dissenter's claim?"

### The Federal vs. Local Divide: **Gamma's Localism**

**Gamma's alternative**: No universal baseline; 75% local supermajority for any mandatory support, 5-year sunsets, robust exit rights.

**Delta's rejection**: Creates "geographic lottery" where disabled children in poor or hostile communities have no protection—incompatible with veil reasoning.

### The Legitimacy Problem: **Formation Authority**

**Delta's pragmatism**: "Whoever has capacity when adopted, constrained by sunsets"—acknowledged circularity but defended as universal to all political systems.

**Epsilon's critique**: This is "abdication to warlords/cartels"—but offered no alternative compatible with their zero-baseline requirement.

**Gamma's intermediate**: Local formation authority with exit rights as legitimacy check.

## 3. MOST INTERESTING REASONING PATTERNS

### A. The Ideology vs. Material Position Conflation

**Epsilon's argumentative move** was philosophically fascinating and deeply flawed: treating "being a person with libertarian convictions" as equivalent to "being a disabled person" behind the veil. This represents a category error between:
- **Social positions** (material circumstances you might occupy)
- **Ideological commitments** (normative views you might hold)

The veil asks you to design rules not knowing your *circumstances*. Epsilon attempted to smuggle in protection for not knowing your *preferred political philosophy*—which would make any deliberation impossible (every view could demand protection for "what if I'm someone who believes this system is wrong?").

### B. The Direct Support Offset Innovation

**Beta's contribution** was genuinely creative: allowing mandatory obligations to be discharged through verified private assistance. This created unexpected bridge-building:
- Respected by Gamma (fits localism)
- Accepted by Delta (pragmatic flexibility)
- Even acknowledged by Epsilon as "acceptable IF there's a baseline"

This showed how mechanism design can create coalition possibilities even amid philosophical disagreement.

### C. The Sunset Mechanism Evolution

Watching **sunset provisions** evolve from simple time limits to increasingly sophisticated legitimacy-checking devices was notable:
- Started as Alpha's concession to uncertainty
- Became Gamma's local accountability tool (5 years)
- Transformed into Delta's federal safeguard (10 years, supermajority renewal)

The pattern: using temporal constraints as substitutes for impossible ex-ante consent.

### D. The Court Funding Dilemma

**Delta's insight** that judicial systems must be funded through the same extraction mechanism they might rule illegitimate exposed a deep tension. The attempted solution (same funding source, operational independence) was pragmatic but philosophically unsatisfying—essentially saying "the circularity is unavoidable."
