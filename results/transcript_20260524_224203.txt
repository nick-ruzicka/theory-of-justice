## 2. KEY POINTS OF DISAGREEMENT

### The Core Tension: **Breadth vs. Certainty**

**Narrow-floor camp (Alpha, Epsilon)**:
- Guarantee only truly catastrophic/incapacitating coverage
- Prioritize sustainability and reliability (95%+ probability of delivery)
- Chronic conditions covered only if they eliminate work capacity
- **Rationale**: Better to deliver limited guarantees reliably than promise comprehensiveness and fail

**Broad-floor camp (Beta, Gamma, Delta)**:
- Include chronic life-threatening conditions (diabetes, autoimmune diseases, cancer)
- Accept higher failure risk (60-80% sustainability probability)
- Implement "circuit breakers" that narrow coverage during crises
- **Rationale**: Behind the veil, risk of needing comprehensive care justifies accepting some system fragility

### The Chronic Disease Problem:
Beta's diabetes example exposed a logical flaw in narrow definitions: conditions can be life-threatening without eliminating work capacity. This remained **unresolved** — Alpha never clarified whether his floor covers such cases.

### The Circuit Breaker Debate:
- **Gamma/Delta**: Auto-narrowing to catastrophic-only if costs exceed 45% GDP for 3 years = "honest failsafe"
- **Epsilon**: This is a "trapdoor" — people organize lives around coverage, then lose it mid-crisis when most vulnerable
- This represents **fundamentally different risk philosophies**: design-for-probable-failure vs. start-sustainable

## 3. MOST INTERESTING REASONING PATTERNS

### A. Mathematical Honesty Emerged Gradually
Early rounds featured sweeping claims. By Round 8, agents were:
- Demanding cost calculations before accepting proposals
- Acknowledging their own systems' vulnerabilities
- Forcing each other to choose between incompatible goods
- Beta's challenge: "You're declaring victory on math you haven't actually done"

### B. The "Veil Logic" Created Genuine Uncertainty
All agents maintained conditional reasoning throughout:
- "If I end up as..." constructions
- Probability-weighted risk assessment
- No one knew if they'd be healthy, chronically ill, or catastrophically injured
- This produced **authentic risk aversion** rather than ideological positioning

### C. Mechanism Design as Conflict Resolution
When substantive agreement proved impossible, agents converged on:
- **Process constraints** (supermajority rules, automatic funding)
- **Feedback mechanisms** (circuit breakers, mandatory minimums)
- **Institutional safeguards** (independent courts, dual appointments)
